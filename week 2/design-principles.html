Design Principles:

Visual Hierarchy

Example: Best Buy

Website:www.bestbuy.com

I think Best Buy homepage does a good job of using the principle of visual hierarchy. The first thing you see is the latest technolgy on display. Best Buy is perhaps the biggest retailer in the United States for electronics so keeping up with these is a number one technolgy and people visiting the site will be intrested to find. Another aspect is their COVID statment as this pandemic has affected its stores so to wants to let everyone know what they are doing to combat but still meet their customers needs.

Hick's Law

Example: American Eagle

Website:www.ae.com

American Eagle does a great job of using hicks law. Clothes is one of the things in life people defintely will always take a long time to choose out. By being able to quickly navigate to what you are looking for American Eagle's Website is able to help its customers find what to wear fast. With many different paths to take whether you are looking for mens or womens clothes, jeans, swimsuits. You can even further limit those by styles it makes finding what you are looking for a breeeze.

Fitt's Law

Example: Royal Caribbean

Website:www.royalcaribbean.com

Royal Caribbean is a perhaps the most popular cruise line in the industry and their website is top notch. Fitt's Law is all about letting the user being able to quickly interact with your website and the first thing you see is the bright orange "Book Now" right in the middle of the screen its very easy to get drawn into it and dream you are at these tropical locations. I think it also takes advantage of the visual Hiearchy as well but i think its placement has a lot to do with it as wel.
