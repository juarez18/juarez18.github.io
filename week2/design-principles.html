Design Principles:

Visual Hierarchy

Example: Best Buy

Website:www.bestbuy.com

I think Best Buy homepage does a good job of using the principle of visual hierarchy. The first thing you see is the latest technology on display. Best Buy is perhaps the biggest retailer in the United States for electronics so keeping up with these is a number one technology and people visiting the site will be interested to find. Another aspect is their COVID statement as this pandemic has affected its stores so to wants to let everyone know what they are doing to combat but still meet their customer's needs.

Hick's Law

Example: American Eagle

Website:www.ae.com

American Eagle does a great job of using hicks law. Clothes are one of the things in life people definitely will always take a long time to choose out. By being able to quickly navigate to what you are looking for American Eagle's Website can help its customers find what to wear fast. With many different paths to take whether you are looking for men's or women's clothes, jeans, swimsuits. You can even further limit those by styles it makes finding what you are looking for a breeze.

Fitt's Law

Example: Royal Caribbean

Website:www.royalcaribbean.com

Royal Caribbean is perhaps the most popular cruise line in the industry and its website is top-notch. Fitt's Law is all about letting the user being able to quickly interact with your website and the first thing you see is the bright orange "Book Now" right in the middle of the screen, it's very easy to get drawn into it and dream you are at these tropical locations. I think it also takes advantage of the visual Hierarchy as well but I think its placement has a lot to do with it as well.
